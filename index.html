Status — v11: First Clean Test of Corpus-Expansion Hypothesis. Still Word Salad. Corpus Volume Is Not the Bottleneck.

Update 2026-05-02: v11_dialog_2corpus deployed live — architecture frozen at v9/v10's 384D / 12L / 12H / RoPE / Pre-LN / AdamW, brain_params=21,509,761. Trained 2026-05-02 03:55 UTC on Modal L4, 11h08m wall-time, ~$6. Vocab grew 9,509 → 14,704 — definitive proof both corpus files were ingested this time (v10's vocab stayed at 9,509 because the second file got dropped due to a commit bug). The corpus-expansion hypothesis was actually tested for the first time.

What was different from v10: the subprocess timeout was bumped from 27,800s → 42,000s (the v11-original failure mode), function timeout from 28,800s → 43,200s, and per-epoch checkpointing was added to ingest.py as defense-in-depth. Both dailydialog_clean.txt and everyday_conversations.txt (5.3 MB combined) were committed and present in the container. Engram N-gram module size doubled (74.4 MB → 147.6 MB) because NGRAM_TABLE_SIZE was also bumped from 50,021 → 100,003.

Loss progression (5 epochs): epoch 1: 1.1084 · epoch 2: 1.0867 · epoch 3: 1.0749 · epoch 4: ~1.057 · epoch 5 (final): 1.0480. Compared to v9/v10 finals around 1.05, v11 lands close — but with 55% more vocab to discriminate. Comparable per-token loss on a harder distribution is consistent with genuine improvement from the expanded corpus.

Eval results (eval_chat.py, 16 prompts — eval_runs/chat_20260502_195749.json): Criterion 1 (distinct outputs) — PASS, 16/16 distinct. Criterion 2 (real English tokens) — partial pass: cleaner grammar fragments emerged, but the everyday-conversations corpus introduced brand and proper-noun leakage: ursa, stevia, breville, peppercorns, polaroid, gluteus, vpn, photoshop, ronaldo, infielders. Criterion 3 (≥3/5 chitchat replies coherent) — FAIL: same word-salad as v9/v10. Fragments like "bot i think", "i can", "i ve period", "you can t a", "i m you" — no coherent sentences.

Sample replies from eval transcript (eval_runs/chat_20260502_195749.json):
USER: "what do you like to do" → BOT: "bot i think stimulation spaceship the i can photoshop deformity the bot stimulation for a"
USER: "tell me about yourself" → BOT: "bot bald is i think the a unknown that carmichael i can photoshop bombs the"

Notable signal — avg_ponder collapsed to 1.0: every prior model showed avg_ponder=3.0 (max), meaning the halt gate always ran the full pondering loop. v11 shows avg_ponder=1.0 — the halt gate now terminates after 1 step confidently. The bigger vocab gives sharper predictions; the model "knows" earlier when it has its answer. Interesting structural change, but it did not translate to coherence.

The corpus-volume hypothesis is refuted. v9 (capacity increase, same corpus) produced word salad. v11 (same arch, +55% vocab) is at the same coherence level. Two of the three obvious levers — model capacity and corpus volume — have now been tested cleanly and neither fixes coherence. The remaining hypothesis (driving v12): the loss function is the root cause. Engram trains with MSE on continuous concept embeddings, predicting the embedding of the next token as a regression. MSE rewards predicting the average of plausible next-tokens, which for grammar produces blurry word salad — exactly what we observe. Standard LMs use cross-entropy on token logits (classification), which forces commitment to a specific token. v12 will test switching to softmax cross-entropy over the existing concept-vocab embeddings.

Evaluation Report — Human Judgement

A 16-prompt evaluation across three difficulty buckets (greetings · chitchat · harder Q&A) was run against v4_rope. Result: all 32 generated replies were near-identical sequences of rare/odd words regardless of input prompt. Example prompt/reply pairs:

[USER] hello
[BOT] you for suavely user resigning to the for impressiveness resigning i you freshener that the

[USER] what is the capital of france
[BOT] that slighted resigning to impressiveness you the i and to resigning the defilements for you

[USER] tell me a story
[BOT] you for suavely user resigning to the for impressiveness resigning i you freshener that the

Diagnosis: the model isn't conditioning on input prompts. The output collapses to the same vocab cluster regardless of context. Two plausible causes:

  • Undertraining — 1 epoch on a corpus where ~50% is 19th-century novels means rare archaic vocab dominates the output distribution. Classic sign of insufficient gradient steps.
  • Eval bug — the bench harness used torch.randn() embeddings in its eval_cosine_top1, which never moved off 5.0% across any architectural change. The metric may be saturated / noise-dominated, but live eval on real ChromaDB embeddings still produces nonsense — so undertraining is the dominant explanation.

Logical Next Steps (updated 2026-05-02)
  1. v12 — test the loss-function hypothesis. Architecture and corpus are exhausted as levers. v9 (capacity increase) and v11 (corpus expansion, cleanly tested) both fail at the same coherence level. The root-cause hypothesis for v12: MSE on embeddings rewards predicting the mean of plausible tokens, which for grammar produces blurry word salad. Standard LMs use cross-entropy on token logits (classification), which forces commitment to a specific token. v12 will switch to softmax cross-entropy over the existing concept-vocab embeddings — no architecture change, no corpus change, just the loss function. This is the cheapest informative experiment remaining.
  2. Confirm the avg_ponder collapse in v12. v11 showed avg_ponder=1.0 where all prior models showed 3.0. This may be a side-effect of the bigger vocab giving sharper softmax distributions, or it may indicate the halt gate converged on a degenerate policy. Monitor in v12 to distinguish.
  3. Do not add more corpus until the loss-function fix is validated. Larger or more varied corpora will not help while MSE is pushing the model toward the mean. The word-salad pattern is consistent with the loss function, not a data problem.
